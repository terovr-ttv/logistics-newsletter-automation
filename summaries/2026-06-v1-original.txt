# MONTHLY EXECUTIVE BRIEFING
## Freight, Logistics & Supply Chain — Month in Review

---

### 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

- **Truck rates are turning the corner.** U.S. Bank/DAT data shows spot rates up 31% YoY in May with contract rates up 9%, even as volumes softened — a sign capacity is tightening structurally after a three-year recession [DC Velocity].
- **Supreme Court hands Trump expanded firing power over independent agencies**, likely sealing the removal of STB member Robert Primus and reshaping rail merger oversight just as the UP–NS deal moves forward [FreightWaves].
- **Ocean carriers are pressing aggressively higher.** Maersk raised 2026 guidance citing strong demand; July 1 brings new BAFs, GRIs, PSSs, and FAK hikes — potentially stacking on top of existing Hormuz-related emergency fuel surcharges [The Loadstar][Transport Topics].
- **FedEx Freight begins life as an independent public company**, pivoting toward higher-margin verticals (data centers, healthcare, grocery, SMB) — a direct challenge to ODFL, Saia, and XPO in premium LTL [Supply Chain Dive].
- **Driver shortage formally reclassified as "structural."** IRU projects 3.86M global driver retirements by 2030; in the U.S., border-state enforcement is intensifying inspections and CDL/immigration checks [The Loadstar][FreightWaves].

### 2. FREIGHT & TRUCKING

**Rates and capacity are inflecting upward.** Spot rates climbed from $1.89/mile in March to $2.14 in May, with contract rates reaching $2.18 — the clearest signal yet that the long freight recession is breaking [DC Velocity]. U.S. Bank framed the move as a "lasting shift, rather than a temporary anomaly."

**FedEx Freight's independent debut** posted Q4 revenue of $2.4B (+4.8% YoY) but operating income fell 67%. CEO John Smith is leaning into a dual priority/economy service model and targeting underpenetrated verticals — most notably data center freight [Supply Chain Dive]. Expect direct competitive pressure on Old Dominion, Saia, and XPO.

**LTL incumbents are doubling down on service differentiation.** Old Dominion continues to lead on quality metrics (99% on-time, 0.5% claims ratio, 16 straight Mastio #1 rankings), while Saia launched its **REV initiative** (Rapid/Expanded/Visible), including a guaranteed 10 a.m. delivery service it claims is the earliest standard offering from any nationwide LTL [FreightWaves]. Saia's Q1 claims ratio also hit 0.50%.

**Yellow Corp postscript:** Courts ruled against the Teamsters under the WARN Act, leaving the 22,000 union workers without relief — closing one of the largest labor chapters in LTL history [The Loadstar].

**Border enforcement is intensifying.** Texas, Arizona, and California reported expanded commercial vehicle operations — Wichita County alone issued 109 equipment citations and impounded 16 trucks in a single op, with 24 detained on immigration grounds [FreightWaves]. Fleets running border lanes should expect heavier OOS rates and CDL scrutiny.

**Driver shortage formally structural.** IRU pegs global vacancies near 3M, with Europe losing 660,000 drivers by 2030. Even during the 2022–23 freight downturn, shortages did not ease — confirming the issue is demographic, not cyclical [The Loadstar].

### 3. SUPPLY CHAIN & LOGISTICS

**Port congestion at a four-year high.** Linerlytica reports 3.7M TEU (11% of the global fleet) tied up waiting for berths. North Asia accounts for 38% of global congestion; Benelux/German delays running up to a week [The Loadstar]. Rotterdam dwell times are at 7 days for exports.

**Rates respond accordingly.** SCFI passed 3,200 points; Shanghai–North Europe is up 12% for 40-ft boxes, transpac east coast rates threatening $8,500/40ft in July. Maersk and Hapag-Lloyd are undercutting at <$6,000 to USWC, which could cap peer rate hikes [The Loadstar].

**Carriers are pouring capacity into East Asia–Australia** as rates surged 30%+ in a month. ANL/OOCL launched ACX; Maersk launched the Qilin service. Far East–Australia spot quotes have doubled YoY [The Loadstar].

**SC Ports closing Leatherman Terminal** (Charleston) August 1, consolidating to Wando and North Charleston amid tariff-driven volume softness — a notable U.S. port capacity decision [DC Velocity].

**Tariff/Middle East cost pressures hitting shippers:** Del Monte flagged $40–45M in headwinds, primarily ocean freight (bunker, war surcharges, inland) [Supply Chain Dive]. J.M. Smucker, by contrast, is seeing mid-single-digit deflation in green coffee, enabling promotional pricing [Supply Chain Dive].

**Container losses doubled in 2025** to 1,478 (MSC Elsa sinking accounted for 43% of total), but still 0.0005% of global flows [FreightWaves][DC Velocity].

### 4. REGULATION & POLICY

**Major executive-branch power shift.** SCOTUS in *Trump v. Slaughter* overruled the 1935 Humphrey's Executor precedent, allowing at-will removal of independent agency members. Direct implication: STB member Robert Primus's lawsuit over his August 2025 firing is unlikely to succeed, giving Trump room to reshape the board ahead of the **UP–NS merger review** — the largest rail consolidation ever [FreightWaves]. Expect faster — but more politicized — STB decisions.

**CBP tightens postal import rules.** Effective July 24, mail imports ≤$2,500 require HTS codes, descriptions, weights and quantities; CBP projects $100M+ in additional duty collection annually. Brokers will see expanded workload as many foreign postal operators need to secure broker relationships [Supply Chain Dive].

**USPS pricing overhaul (July 12).** Dimensional divisor drops from 166 to 139 (matching UPS/FedEx), fractional inches round up, and ounce-based Ground Advantage Commercial pricing is eliminated for sub-pound packages. Lightweight/bulky shippers face the biggest hit [Supply Chain Dive].

**Tariff refunds emerging as Q2 tailwind.** Treasury refunded $22B in May after SCOTUS struck IEEPA tariffs; ~40 companies discussed refunds in Q1 but only 8 booked them. Wells Fargo expects a broader pickup in Q2 [Transport Topics].

**Polestar effectively shut out of U.S. market** under connected-vehicle rules targeting Chinese-linked tech; will pivot to Europe [Transport Topics]. Signals continued tightening on Chinese-origin automotive/EV components.

### 5. TECHNOLOGY & INNOVATION

**Autonomous and humanoid robotics activity accelerating:**
- **Agility Robotics announced a SPAC merger** (Churchill Capital XI) to go public as "AGLT," targeting up to $620M to scale Digit humanoid robots already deployed at GXO, Schaeffler, and Toyota [DC Velocity].
- **Uber–Waymo Phoenix pilot wound down**, but Uber is partnering with a new AV provider; Waymo continues expanding solo across 10+ cities [Transport Topics].
- **Tesla settled** the fatal 2023 Arizona FSD pedestrian crash lawsuit; NHTSA investigation continues [Transport Topics].

**Freight tech focus on trust/fraud and automation:**
- **Numeo AI** is positioning as broker trust infrastructure with continuous carrier verification (5,000+ dispatchers, 10,000+ drivers on platform) — a direct response to escalating broker fraud [FreightWaves].
- **myMechanic launched Dealer-Connect** to digitize roadside repair dispatch — average breakdown resolution is currently 4+ hours [FreightWaves].
- **VesselBot's "digital twin" benchmarking** is giving shippers ammunition to push back on carrier BAFs, with one case showing carrier BAFs 55–61% above modeled actual fuel cost [The Loadstar].

**Warehouse automation continues to compound.** HelloFresh expanded Locus Robotics deployment from 100 to 500 chilled SKUs [Supply Chain Dive]; Pandora rolled out Hardis WMS globally, cutting high-severity incidents ~50% [DC Velocity]. Bringg research shows 70%+ AI adoption in last-mile routing but only ~14% investment in billing reconciliation/carrier management — the workflows where margin is actually leaking.
